# Post 1: My Journey in Programming

## Why I Started This Portfolio 

I recently started building my GitHub portfolio as a way to document my journey as a Computer Engineering student. This idea was suggested by Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who teaches Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus. At first, I did not fully understand why maintaining a digital footprint was important, but as the semester progressed, it slowly began to make sense.
## What I Learned
This portfolio is not about highlighting achievements or showing perfect results. Instead, it focuses on the process of learning — the confusion, mistakes, challenges, and gradual improvements. During this semester, I realized that learning is not linear. There were many moments where I felt stuck and unsure, but those same moments turned out to be the most valuable in shaping my understanding.
In Programming Fundamentals, everything felt unfamiliar in the beginning. Even simple concepts required time to understand. Similarly, Database Systems introduced structured thinking that required more than just memorization. Writing about these experiences helps me reflect on how my thinking evolved over time.
Dr. Bilal Ahmad often emphasizes understanding over grades and encourages students to think beyond exams. That mindset influenced me to take this portfolio seriously. Instead of just completing assignments, I started focusing on what I was actually learning.

## Conclusion
This portfolio is just the beginning. In the upcoming posts, I will share specific experiences from my semester, including the struggles I faced, the lessons I learned, and how my approach to learning changed over time.
