# POST 5 : Learning Logic Building

One of the most difficult aspects of Programming Fundamentals was learning how to think logically. Initially, I believed that programming was mainly about writing correct syntax, but I soon realized that logic is the foundation of everything.
Breaking a problem into smaller steps was not easy. I often struggled to figure out where to start and how to proceed. Even simple problems felt complicated because I did not know how to structure my thinking.
Dr. Bilal Ahmad focused heavily on problem-solving, which gradually helped me improve. I started practicing writing logic in plain language before converting it into code.
With time, I began to understand how loops, conditions, and variables work together. Problems that once seemed difficult became easier to approach.
This experience taught me that programming is not about writing code quickly, but about thinking clearly and logically. This lesson will remain important for me in future courses as well.
