# POST 11 :  Exam Week Experience 

The last week of exams was one of the most hectic experiences of my semester. There were multiple assignments pending along with quizzes from different subjects including DLD, Database Systems, and OOP. Each day felt rushed, and I barely had time to properly rest or even eat.
Everything seemed to happen at once. I had to move quickly from one task to another without having enough time to focus properly on any single thing. This created a lot of pressure and made even simple tasks feel difficult.
Looking back, I realize that the main reason for this situation was my lack of early preparation. I had delayed many tasks, thinking I would manage them later. However, when everything accumulated, it became overwhelming.
During this time, I also reflected on how courses like Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems require consistent effort. Concepts like normalization cannot be understood overnight, and programming skills cannot be developed in a short time.
Dr. Bilal Ahmad often advised us to stay consistent and avoid last-minute preparation. At that time, I did not fully follow that advice, but this experience made me understand its importance.
This phase taught me a very important lesson: discipline and time management are essential. In the future, I plan to stay more organized and avoid delaying tasks.
