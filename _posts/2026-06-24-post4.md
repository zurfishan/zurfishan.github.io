# POST 4 : My First Coding Mistake

One of the earliest lessons I learned in programming came from making mistakes. My first program did not work, and I had no idea what went wrong. At that moment, it felt frustrating and discouraging.
I spent a lot of time trying to find the error. It turned out to be something very small, but fixing it required patience. This experience introduced me to debugging, which is an essential part of programming.
Dr. Bilal Ahmad encouraged us to analyze our mistakes instead of avoiding them. That advice helped me develop a better approach. Instead of getting frustrated, I started reading my code more carefully.
This experience changed my perspective. I realized that mistakes are not failures. They are opportunities to learn and improve. Every error helped me understand programming a little better.
From that point on, I became more comfortable with making mistakes. Instead of fearing them, I started seeing them as part of the learning process.
