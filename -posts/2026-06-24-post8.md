## Introduction to Database Systems 
After spending time on Programming Fundamentals, starting Database Systems felt like entering a completely different area of study. Instead of focusing on logic and flow, this course emphasized how data is organized and managed.
At first, the concepts felt theoretical. Terms like tables, attributes, and relationships seemed simple, but understanding how they connect required attention. I realized that managing data efficiently is a critical part of building software systems.
Dr. Bilal Ahmad explained how databases are used in real-world applications, which helped me understand their importance. Instead of seeing it as just another subject, I started viewing it as a practical skill.
One interesting aspect was how structured everything is in databases. Unlike programming, where logic flows step by step, databases require proper organization from the beginning.
This course helped me understand that data is at the core of most systems. Learning how to manage it properly is just as important as writing code.
