## POST 9 — Struggles with Normalization

Normalization was one of the most challenging topics for me in Database Systems. It required careful thinking and a clear understanding of relationships between data.
At first, I struggled to differentiate between normal forms. Concepts like functional dependencies and removing redundancy were difficult to grasp. Even when I understood the theory, applying it correctly was not easy.
I often found myself making mistakes while identifying keys or breaking tables into proper forms. This made the process slow and sometimes frustrating.
However, with consistent practice, things started becoming clearer. Dr. Bilal Ahmad emphasized understanding the logic behind normalization instead of memorizing steps. This approach helped me improve gradually.
Over time, I realized that normalization is not just a theoretical concept. It plays an important role in designing efficient databases. It helps reduce redundancy and improves data integrity.
This experience taught me that difficult topics require patience and repeated practice. Understanding comes slowly, but it stays longer.
