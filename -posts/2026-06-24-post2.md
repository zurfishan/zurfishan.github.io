# Post 2: My First Day of Programming Fundamentals 

The first day of Programming Fundamentals was very different from what I had imagined. I entered the class thinking I would simply learn how to write code, but it quickly became clear that the course was more about learning how to think logically.
Dr. Bilal Ahmad introduced programming as a problem-solving skill rather than just a technical subject. At that moment, I did not fully understand this perspective. I assumed syntax and coding rules would be the hardest part, but over time I realized that logic building was the real challenge.
Everything felt new and confusing. Concepts like variables, data types, and input-output seemed simple at first, but applying them correctly was difficult. I often found myself struggling to understand how different parts of a program connect together.
There were moments where I felt lost during lectures, but I continued trying to follow along. Gradually, I began to understand that programming requires patience. It is not something that can be learned instantly
## Conclusion
That first day gave me an important realization: programming is a skill that develops slowly through practice and consistency. This mindset helped me stay patient during the early phase of learning and prepared me for the challenges ahead.
