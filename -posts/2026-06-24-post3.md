## POST 3 — My Fear of Programming

Before even starting Programming Fundamentals, I already had a fear of coding. I had heard many people say that programming is difficult, and that idea stayed in my mind. Because of this, I approached the subject with hesitation.
When I started writing my first programs, even small mistakes felt overwhelming. A missing semicolon or incorrect logic could cause the entire program to fail. These small issues made me question whether I was capable of learning programming at all.
During this phase, mindset played a very important role. Dr. Bilal Ahmad always emphasized understanding concepts instead of rushing through them. This approach helped me slowly overcome my fear.
Instead of aiming for perfection, I began focusing on progress. I started taking small steps, trying to understand each concept before moving forward. This reduced my anxiety and made learning more manageable.
Over time, I realized that programming is not something to fear. It is a skill that improves with practice. This shift in mindset was one of the most important turning points in my learning journey.
